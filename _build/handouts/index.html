Correlation Trials

Introduction

Correlating two values in a dataset is an important part of your data analysis toolset. Not only is important to know how to determine correlation between variables, but also if the correlation between variables is meaningful and/or appropriate to include in your analysis.

Setup

Refer to the correlation functions present in the video “Demo: Calculating and Visualizing Correlation” paying close attention to how to determine whether a correlation is statistically significant.

Create a new Jupyter notebook environment and install the following packages:

  • notebook

  • pandas

  • numpy

  • scipy

Download or copy the Absenteeism Dataset we used in the last exercise into your project directory.

Trials

Directions

Calculate the correlation coefficient between the following pairs of variables using the absenteeism dataset. For each calculation, add an explanation that includes

  • Whether there is a strong correlation

  • Whether the correlation is statistically significant

  • What the relationship between the two variables means, regardless of strong/weak correlation

    • How would you use this in a presentation about this dataset? How would you explain the correlation to someone who is not familiar with the dataset?

In addition, use each of the following correlation calculation methods at least once.

  • Scipy’s spearmanr

  • Scipy’s pearsonr

  • Numpy’s corrcoef

Problems

  1. Day of the week and reason for absence

  2. Month of absence and age

  3. Hit target and disciplinary failure

  4. Service time and reason for absence

  5. Transportation expense and distance from residence to work

  6. Age and pet

  7. Absenteeism time in hours and day of the week

  8. Month of absence and absenteeism time in hours

  9. Body mass index and height

  10. Pet and disciplinary failure

When you have finished, push your Jupyter notebook to Github to turn in this exercise.
